Causes of Oil Pipeline Spill by Pipeline Type (2010-2017)

  • This spider chart visualizes the different causes of oil pipeline spills based on pipeline type (aboveground, underground, and tank) from 2010 to 2017.
  • The chart provides a clear overview of the different causes and their impact on pipeline spills.
  • For aboveground pipelines, the most significant cause of spills was other outside force damage, with a value of 273.74. For tank pipelines, the most significant cause of spills was all other causes, with a value of 137.88. Finally, for underground pipelines, the most significant cause of spills was material/weld/equip failure, with a value of 212.69.
  • The chart highlights the importance of addressing the different causes of spills and implementing safety measures to prevent such incidents in the future.
